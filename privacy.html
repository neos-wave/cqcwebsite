1. Who we are

This service is provided by Neos Wave Ltd ("Neos Wave", "we", "us", "our"), a company registered in England and Wales under company number [company number], with its registered office at [registered address]. Neos Wave Ltd is the contracting party for the Vantage platform and is registered with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) under registration number [ICO reg number].

This policy explains how we handle personal data when you visit our website, enquire about or use Vantage (the "Platform"), or otherwise interact with us. You can contact us about privacy at privacy@neoswave.works.

2. Our role: controller and processor

Whether we are a "controller" or a "processor" depends on the data in question. This distinction matters, especially where care records are involved.

SituationOur roleWhat it means
Website visitors, enquiries, marketing, and the accounts of the staff who use the PlatformControllerWe decide why and how this data is processed, as described in this policy.
Care, compliance and evidence data that a customer (a care provider) enters into, or connects to, the Platform — which may include information about staff and residentsProcessorThe care provider is the controller. We process this data only on their documented instructions, under a Data Processing Agreement (DPA).

Where we act as a processor, the care provider's own privacy notice governs that data. Residents and their families should contact the relevant care provider to exercise their rights; we will support the provider in responding.

3. The personal data we collect

As a controller

  • Contact & enquiry data: name, work email, telephone number, organisation, role, and anything you include in a message or demo request.
  • Account data: the login details and profile of staff who use the Platform, and records of their activity within it for security and support.
  • Usage & technical data: IP address, device and browser type, pages viewed and navigation paths, collected to operate and improve the website and Platform.

As a processor (on behalf of customers)

  • Compliance & evidence data submitted by, or connected from, the customer's existing systems. Depending on how the customer uses the Platform, this may include personal data and special category data (such as health information) relating to staff and residents. The customer controls what is provided.

Where we rely on legitimate interests, we have balanced those interests against your rights and freedoms. You can ask us about this balancing test at any time.

5. Special category (health and care) data

Care records can include special category data. Where such data is present in the Platform, it is provided and controlled by the care provider, and we process it only as their processor under a DPA, applying the technical and organisational safeguards described below. We do not use this data for our own purposes, and we never sell it.

6. How the Platform's AI processes data

The Platform uses artificial intelligence to structure and surface evidence that practitioners have provided — for example, organising it against the relevant regulatory framework and flagging gaps. It is designed to support a registered manager's judgement, with a human reviewing outputs. To deliver this, relevant content may be processed by trusted AI sub-processors under contractual confidentiality and data-protection terms. We do not permit customer data to be used to train third-party public models. A current list of sub-processors is available on request: privacy@neoswave.works.

7. Who we share data with

We do not sell personal data. We share it only with:

  • Service providers (sub-processors) who help us run the Platform — for example UK-based cloud hosting, AI processing, analytics and email delivery — under contracts that require appropriate protection.
  • Professional advisers and authorities where required by law, regulation or to protect our rights.
  • A successor entity in the event of a reorganisation, merger or sale, subject to this policy.

8. International transfers

We aim to host and process personal data in the UK. Where any processing takes place outside the UK, we ensure an appropriate safeguard is in place — such as UK adequacy regulations or the International Data Transfer Agreement / Addendum — so your data receives an equivalent level of protection.

9. How long we keep it

As a controller, we keep personal data only as long as needed for the purposes above, then delete or anonymise it: enquiry data for up to [e.g. 24 months] after last contact, and account data for the life of the account plus [retention period]. As a processor, we retain customer data per the DPA, and return or delete it on request or when the contract ends.

10. How we protect it

We apply appropriate technical and organisational measures — including encryption in transit and at rest, access controls, least-privilege permissions, logging and regular review — aligned to the NHS Data Security and Protection Toolkit. No internet transmission is ever completely secure, but we work to protect your data and to manage any incident in line with our legal obligations.

11. Cookies

We use essential cookies to operate the site and, with your consent, analytics cookies to understand usage. You can manage non-essential cookies through our cookie banner or your browser settings. Full detail is in our Cookie Policy [link].

12. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to access your data; to have inaccurate data corrected; to erasure; to restrict or object to processing; to data portability; and to withdraw consent at any time. To exercise these rights as they relate to data we control, contact privacy@neoswave.works. For data we process on behalf of a care provider, please contact that provider, who is the controller.

13. Children

Our website and the Platform are intended for care-sector professionals and are not directed at children. We do not knowingly collect data directly from children through the website. Any information about young people held within a customer's care records is controlled by that customer.

14. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The "last updated" date above shows when it last changed, and we will tell customers about material changes that affect them.

15. Contact and complaints

For any privacy question, contact our data protection lead at privacy@neoswave.works or write to Neos Wave Ltd, [registered address]. If you are unhappy with how we have handled your data, you can complain to the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk — though we'd appreciate the chance to put things right first.
